Getting to Pittsburgh

If you will be driving to Pittsburgh for our wedding, we recommend simply using Google Maps. Please see the How do I get there section

If you'll be flying in, Pittsburgh International Airport (PIT) is your best choice. Situated just 25 minutes west of the city, it offers a convenient gateway to our celebration. Upon arrival, transportation to your hotel or our wedding venue is a breeze, with taxis, Uber, or Lyft readily available.

If you're making the journey to Pittsburgh from Philadelphia, we highly recommend considering Amtrak as a great alternative to driving. Amtrak offers daily service connecting the two cities, providing a scenic and stress-free travel experience. Not only can it often be more cost-effective when you factor in tolls and fuel costs, but you'll also have the opportunity to relax and take in the picturesque landscapes of Pennsylvania along the way. Sit back, enjoy the journey, and even catch up on work or simply unwind during your comfortable train ride.

Things to do

Between the ceremony and reception there will be some time to relax or have a drink nearby. Here's some of the places we love.

Bars and Restaurants
  • Carson St. Deli - Best sandwiches and craft beer selection in the city.
  • Hofbräuhaus - An iconic German biergarten experience overlooking the Monongahela river.
  • Acacia - Speakeasy known for craft cocktails and a warm atmosphere.
  • Dee's Cafe - A classic old school dive bar with pool and ice cold Pabst Blue Ribbon on tap. (Note! Smoking inside is permitted)
  • Tiki Lounge - Retro recreation of a high-style Tiki bar. Complete with waterfalls, flaming cocktails, leis, and fun.
  • Carmella's Plates and Pints - Upscale rustic whiskey bar.
Activities
  • Mt. Washington overlook and Inclines - Take a ride up the iconic Pittsburgh inclines and get a unique view of the city.
  • Three Rivers Heritage Trail - Nice walking trail along the Monongahela river.
  • Color Park - Vibrant area along the Three Rivers Heritage Trail with funky art and a beautiful view.
  • Armstrong Field & Park - A playground and open field to relax or play with your children.
  • Pins Mechanical - Oldschool fun including duckpin bowling, pinball, and arcade.
  • Southside Works - Explore local boutiques and shops. (Hofbräuhaus and Pins Mechanical also located here)

If you'll be staying for the weekend and have some more time, here are some of our favorite places to go.
  • Phipps Conservatory and Botanical Gardens - Beautiful and historic indoor/outdoor gardens.
  • Mattress Factory - Contemporary art museum. Nearby Randyland another famous Pittsburgh eclectic art installation.
  • Carnegie Museums of Art and Natural History - Feature impressive collections spanning art and natural sciences.
  • Point State Park - Scenic urban oasis at the confluence of rivers, offering stunning views and a historical focal point for Pittsburgh's heritage.
  • Strip District - A bustling, cultural neighborhood in Pittsburgh renowned for its vibrant markets, diverse eateries, and unique shops. Perfect place to go on Sunday morning.

Where is the Church Parking?
Church address is 160 S 15th St, Pittsburgh, PA 15203

There are two available parking lots for St. Adalbert Church

1. 110 South 14th St. Pittsburgh PA, 15203. This parking lot is at the rear entrance of the church.

2. Mary Queen of Peach Parish parking lot. 81 S 13th St, Pittsburgh, PA 15203. This parking lot is located two blocks from the church. It is about a 5 minute walk.

As a last resort there is also street parking in the area.

Parking space is limited, so please carpool if you are able to.

Where is the Reception Parking?

Parking for the reception will be available at the Fobes parking garage at 1180 Forbes Ave, Pittsburgh, PA 15219

Parking validation tickets will be available at the reception.

Once you park, take the elevator to the 8th floor where you can walk across the skywalk to the ballroom.

How do I get to the Reception?

There are two entrances to the Power Center.

1. Forbes Avenue Entrance. This is the entrance you will most likely be taking if you are walking from the hotel. Walk into the Power Center and take the elevator to the 5th floor.

2. University Entrance. This is the entrance you will most likely be taking if you are parking in the Forbes parking garage. Once you park, take the elevator to the 8th floor. From there walk across the skywalk.
